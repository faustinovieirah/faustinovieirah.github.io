About me

Email: faustinovieirah@cardiff.ac.uk

Olá! I am Helena Faustino Vieira, originally from Câmara de Lobos in Madeira, Portugal. I moved to the UK in 2016 for my Master's degree in Physics, Astrophysics and Cosmology, where I've graduated with distinction. During my undergraduate career I dabbled in several research areas, such as performing my own observations of exoplanet transits to analysing the kinematic alignment (or misalignment) of hot gas and stars in quenched galaxies.

Currently I am doing my PhD at Cardiff University under Ana Duarte-Cabral and Tim Davis. My thesis consists of analysing the star-forming molecular gas in nearby galaxies, through a novel dust extinction mapping technique. For more details, please check my research and publications sections.

Projects

FFOGG (Following the Flow of Gas in Galaxies)

I am part of the core team of the FFOGG project, which is focused on understanding the physical processes dictating the flow of gas in galaxies from the diffuse, large-scale interstellar medium (ISM) to dense star-forming regions. We want to investigate how stars form within the galactic context, and establish a link between the large-scale environment and the small-scale physics of star formation. For that purpose, we naturally need information that spans many orders of magnitude in terms of physical scale and densities.

The FFOGG project aims to do just that: understand how spiral galaxies perform as star formation engines. To do this, we link both high-resolution observations and sophisticated simulations of the Milky Way and nearby spiral galaxies. I am leading the nearby galaxy observational side of this project. Please visit the FFOGG website for more details on our work, and for a list of related publications and available data products.
